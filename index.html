Human Sex Trafficking: A Global Epidemic

Human sex trafficking is defined as modern sexual slavery. The FBI places the number of sex trafficking victims both domestic and international in the millions. Young females and children are the most common victims of this trade though there are some male victims as well. Sex traffickers lure their victims by promising certain things like job opportunities, helping the victim’s families financially, and providing emotional support especially with young naïve girls and teenage runaways. The truth is that these traffickers deceive their victims and then through abuse, force, and coercion force their victims into prostitution. Traffickers also known as pimps and madams threaten their victims by beating them into submission, threatening their families, raping them repeatedly, controlling them though drug use, etc. Trafficking victims live in deplorable conditions with rancid rooms, putrid mattresses, and signs of drug use everywhere. Human sex trafficking is a big business that makes at least 32 billion per year. Only drug trafficking is a bigger criminal enterprise. Sex trafficking exists within the broader commercial sex trade, often at much larger rates than most people realize or understand. Sex trafficking has been found in a wide variety of venues of the overall sex industry, including residential brothels, hostess clubs, online escort services, fake massage businesses, strip clubs, and street prostitution.

Michael, an individual who believes in freedom for all has teamed up with the Polaris Project, a non-profit organization whose vision is for a world without slavery to raise awareness of the horrors of human sex trafficking and what you can do to stop it and help save our children from this evil trade.

Sex Trafficking Survivors

Tina Frundt

Tina Frundt is a leading figure in the crusade to help children sexually exploited for commercial purposes. Ms. Frundt herself a survivor of domestic sex trafficking; now dedicates her life to helping women and children heal from domestic sex trafficking and commercial sex exploitation. She is the founder of Courtney's House which supports sex trafficking victims and helps them to recover from their ordeal.

Somaly Mam

The Somaly Mam Foundation (SMF) is a nonprofit organization dedicated to the eradication of sex slavery and the empowerment of its survivors, led by the vision and life's work of Cambodian survivor and activist Somaly Mam. Founded in 2007, the organization's multilateral approach helps victims to escape their plight, empowers survivors with economic independence and as part of the solution, and engages governments, corporations, and individuals in the fight.
