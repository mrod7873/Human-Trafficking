Justice for the Victims

U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement


U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement is a part of Homeland Security that investigates human trafficking networks both domestically and internationally. ICE coordinates with other law enforcement agencies such as Interpol and Europol to bring sex traffickers to justice and rescue the victims. ICE has also launched various campaigns such as the Hidden in Plain Sight campaign whose goal is to draw the American public's attention to the plight of human-trafficking victims in the United States. The campaign message explains that human trafficking includes those who are sexually exploited or forced to work against their will. ICE’s mission is to dismantle the sex trafficking networks and provide support for the victims when they are rescued.

Sex Trafficking Statistics

The FBI places the number of sex trafficking victims both domestic and international in the millions. According to FBI statistics on human sex trafficking, the average age of female victims is around 12 to 14 years old. For boys and transgendered youth, it’s around 11 to 13 years old. Sex trafficking is also becoming a growing problem in the US with the CIA estimating that between 45,000-50,000 victims are being trafficked each year in the US alone with the most common victims being teenage runaways. UNICEF estimates that the global market of child sex trafficking is estimated to be worth over $12 billion a year. These statistics show a disturbing trend where countless youth are being exploited by sex traffickers every day for huge profits at expense of the victims.
